# AuditFlow – Product Requirements Document

## 1. Product Overview

AuditFlow is an AI-powered governance analytics platform that tracks public fund utilization, identifies financial anomalies, and improves transparency in government spending.

The system monitors how government funds move from central authorities to states, districts, and individual projects.

The goal is to create a digital platform that transforms financial tracking from simple transaction monitoring into an intelligent auditing and transparency system.

---

# 2. Problem Statement

Government financial systems primarily act as transaction ledgers. They record that funds were transferred between departments but do not verify whether those funds produced real-world infrastructure or services.

This creates multiple systemic issues:

• Funds remain idle in administrative accounts  
• Infrastructure projects may exist only on paper  
• Contractors may inflate procurement costs  
• Departments rush spending near fiscal deadlines  

These problems lead to inefficient use of public resources and reduced public trust.

---

# 3. Product Vision

To build a unified financial intelligence platform that connects government financial records with real-world infrastructure progress.

The platform should help:

• Governments monitor budget utilization  
• Auditors detect corruption early  
• Engineers track project progress  
• Citizens understand public spending  

---

# 4. Objectives

The system should achieve the following goals:

1. Provide real-time visualization of public fund flow.
2. Monitor project budget utilization.
3. Detect financial anomalies using machine learning.
4. Provide transparency to citizens.
5. Reduce inefficient or fraudulent spending.

---

# 5. Target Users

## Government Administrators

Responsible for monitoring national and state-level fund allocation.

Needs:
• macro budget overview
• fund flow analytics
• reallocation insights

---

## Auditors

Responsible for financial verification and compliance.

Needs:
• anomaly alerts
• suspicious transaction detection
• contractor network insights

---

## Project Engineers

Responsible for managing infrastructure projects.

Needs:
• project progress tracking
• budget monitoring
• milestone reporting

---

## Citizens

Interested in transparency and accountability.

Needs:
• view local government projects
• verify infrastructure claims
• report issues

---

# 6. Core Features

## Fund Flow Visualization

The platform will visualize the movement of funds between administrative levels.

Example flow:

Central Government → State → District → Project

This will be shown using Sankey diagrams and charts.

---

## Budget Utilization Monitoring

Each project will display:

• allocated budget
• total expenditure
• remaining budget
• spending efficiency

Burn-rate analytics will compare time elapsed vs budget spent.

---

## AI-Based Anomaly Detection

Machine learning models will detect abnormal financial patterns.

Examples:

• unusually high invoice amounts
• sudden spending spikes
• contractor dominance patterns

---

## Project Verification

Engineers can upload project progress updates including:

• images
• descriptions
• location data

---

## Citizen Transparency Portal

Citizens can search for government projects by location and view details about budget allocation and project status.

They can also report infrastructure issues.

---

# 7. Success Metrics

Success of the platform will be measured using:

• reduction in idle government funds
• faster audit processes
• increased transparency
• number of anomalies detected
• citizen engagement metrics
